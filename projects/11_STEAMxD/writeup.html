Introduction to STEAMxD

The STEAMxD workshop is an interdisciplinary workshop held by SUTD and MOE. It is meant to apply a design systems approach to existing STEAM technologies. This bridges the technological components (technical skills for prototyping) and design components (human-centric skills and social considerations for understanding) for a better outcome.

The Workshops

I participated in the AI Rescue Robot track in 2024. The overall task is to create a robot that is able to recognise and rescue casualties in natural disasters. The two deliverables were a presentation about the design of a robot and why that particular design is appropriate, and a practical portion on a AI image classifier using a camera mounted on a remote-controlled robot.

We were exposed to the design thinking and social-ethical reasoning, such as a prioritisation problem and creating problem, activity, interaction, and information scenarios. For the technical portions, we were trained on Computer-Aided Design to 3D print a mountable adapter for our camera, robot assembly, and AI image recognition.

Presentation of Robot Design

The design thinking modules were helpful in this aspect. We were given frameworks to consider various persona such as elderly, young children, and adults, as well as their unique needs. This then allowed us to scope our presentation to target these people more directly.

Our proposal were small spheres that are able to manoeuvre more easily into tiny crevices after earthquacks. Their main aim is to recognise trapped people and provide essentials such as water and food until larger scale equipment is able to rescue them. This helps to complement existing solutions.

Image Classification using Robot-Mounted Camera

We were also guided through making a YOLOv5 classifier model that would draw bounding boxes and classify the figurines in a picture. We took pictures of figurine cat, dogs, rabbits, and humans, and subsequently labelled the data. We then trained and tested the model iteratively, varying the parameters, increasing the training data and more to increase the accuracy to refine the process.

On the actual day, we had to drive the robot around a obstacle course with multiple of these figurines, taking pictures for the classifier to demonstrate its abilities in varying backgrounds.

Reflections

In the end, we won the Most Innovative Solution award. Overall, the programme was enjoyable and eye-opening. It was greatly helpful to be introduced to frameworks such as persona generation and storyboarding. Rather than go off a vague idea of what design thinking is, these would help me to better scope my projects to ensure I give sufficient consideration to the human users. This programme also contextualised all these steps from start to finish in a guided manner which was a very useful experience.
