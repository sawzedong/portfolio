Overview of Science Lab Internship

Following my internship at DSTA, I also had another month before JC was due to start. Hence, to further pursue my interest in research, I took on an in-house internship that was offerred by my school lab. The lab, DHS Molecular Lab, was the lab that had housed my year-long SMP endeavour, and hence it was truly meaningful for me to also intern to gain an even deeper appreciation of the research processes.

My Experience

I took on this internship with two other students. During this phase, we mainly helped to test out novel research ideas for different groups. For our juniors who were looking to start their own research projects, we helped to first collect some preliminary data to determine if their research project may be feasible. This can allow them to more accurately scope their projects or shift a focus if a more appropriate idea is found. The lab technician would let us know about the ideas that the students were interested in carrying out research in, giving us a variety of topics to also focus in ourselves.

The topics that I focused on included testing bacterial inhibition of papain, removing heavy metal ions, and recovering enzymes from spent mushroom compost to remove UV filters, a waste product and pollutant from sunblock. Furthermore, I also had the chance to expand on my Science Mentorship Programme research, conducting a few more rounds of protoplast fusion even if it was ultimately unsuccessful.

Reflections

This was also really enjoyable as each day was quite a new challenge—while there were some longer projects that required a few days to run, the focus of each day could be really different as there were a few projects to focus on. This gave me a broad scope of understanding that was not so limited and I enjoyed the novelty of these different projects. By helping out with stocktake and other administrative matters, it also helped me to appreciate the effort required by the lab to sustain all our projects.

This internship also helped to train both my experimentation skills and my communication skills to my mentor about the results. It also helped me gain an even deeper appreciation of the work that went into making my own research possible, and I am thankful for that.

Disclaimer: As these works were done years in the past, I do not necessarily believe these are my best works and I recognise their limitations. However, they remain available for reference and also as a way to chart my growth!
