Overview of Y3 Orientation

Students will choose their subject combination and shuffle their classes between Secondary 2 and 3, meeting many new faces in their classes. Hence, the school initiated holding an orientation at Secondary 3. Being part of the team of CCA student leaders, I was given the chance and volunteered to help plan this orientation.

My Role and Contribution

I was grateful to be able to serve as one of two finale sub-committee leaders. Together, we led our team in preparing the videos for the storyline, the publicity, and the grand finale in which we would also crown classes as winners. As sub-committee leaders, we also pitched in on the overall logistics, coordination, and the storyline, ensuring a smooth flow of the event.

While I do not have much experience in creative direction, we still came together to film small skits and videos to play into the storyline and immerse the students in it. This included writing scripts, making props as well as getting some of our friends to be actors. Some of these clips were also used as publicity to get the Secondary 3 students excited for the orientation.

For the finale, it was a unique challenge to plan as this was held during COVID-19. As such, while the finale had to be conducted over Zoom with each class staying in their classrooms, we still did our best to energise the students and give them a memorable finale. We were able to screen the ending to the storyline, judge some of their class talent submissions, play a montage of the past few days, and finally crown the winners of the orientation.

Reflections

Y3 Orientation was one of the events that I deeply enjoyed planning. While it presented its own challenges, that made the orientation planning all the more fun as we tried to still give the Y3s a fun start to the year while navgiating the unique circumstances. I was also very grateful to have a very capable partner and a committed team that I collaborated well with.

This event led me to use my strengths, applying it in different ways and new contexts to still achieve the final goal. It helped me to better appreciate the coordination required for planning such events, as well as teaching me proposal and presentation skills. Planning this event with my friends was a fulfilling experience, and seeing the ice being broken within the classes and smiles on their faces made the hours spent worth it.
