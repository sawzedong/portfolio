Overview of Science Mentorship Programme (SMP)

SMP is a research programme organised by my school to develop aspiring researchers. In this programme, I could work in groups under a teacher mentor. I can research on a topic of their choice or one advised by the lab technician, who helps to also oversee and provide guidance to the groups. I was able to also carry out experiments using the equipment provided by my school laboratory.

My Research Journey

I was able to carry out research on the topic of "Optimisation of desirable attributes of mycelium and their fruiting bodies through protoplast isolation, fusion, and regeneration" as part of a group of three. This topic was one that was not easy to replicate, but already had existing literature, making it a manageable project given our relative lack of experience. Furthermore, we had chosen specific strains of mushrooms, with well-known benefits on their own, to fuse together. These strains had not been fused together before, and we wanted to investigate if the resultant strain was able to attain the positive attributes of both parent strains.

Through the process, we faced many setbacks. For example, the fusion process had to be repeated multiple times as it was unsuccessful. Furthermore, we had trouble replicating certain assays we were using, making it difficult to accurately assess if our experiments have been successful or not. However, with each iteration, we were able to continually identify the issue that limited our progress, and worked hard to rectify it the next round.

In the end, we were not able to successfully complete the prototplast fusion. However, we pivoted our research paper to still deliver insights based on the results we obtained. We pushed through with the various assays that helped us to prove the value of these mushroom strains, such as antioxidant ability. I also had the chance to put my computing skills to use, creating a simple script to sieve for valid DNA sequences to create primers to test if the mushroom strains we had isolated were indeed the correct strains.

Reflections

SMP gave me a chance to stengthen my experimentation and practical skills, requiring me to be able to not just know if my experiments succeeded or not, but to reflect on what led to the success and failure. I tried to always consider all the possible case scenarios to strive to better understand the experiments, not just for results. Furthermore, these also improved my scientific communication skills. It also spurred my interest in research in the scientific domain to help to discover new knowledge.

Disclaimer: As these works were done years in the past, I do not necessarily believe these are my best works and I recognise their limitations. However, they remain available for reference and also as a way to chart my growth!
