Introduction to Swift Accelerator Programme

The Swift Accelerator Programme (SAP) is a one-year programme ran by Tinkertanker and supported by Apple. It aims to teach students the fundamentals of app creation using the Swift programming language, focusing on both technical coding skills in Swift as well as the process of app creation (e.g. ideation, UI/UX design).

My Experience

I took part in SAP in 2020, a period in which the lessons were mostly conducted over an online format due to COVID-19. Throughout the lessons, I had opportunities to create various small apps to learn basic concepts and skills in Swift and Xcode. These gave me the opportunity to test various elements often seen in apps and the logic behind them.

The programme culminated in a group project where, as part of a team of four, I was able to develop any idea into an iOS app with feedback and mentorship from the teaching team. Our team decided to create a Fridge - Track your food, a fridge management app. This app helps to record items bought and stored in fridges, monitor their shelf lives, and recommend recipes to help utilise these items.

Reflections

While the final app was simple, it was a good experience for me to experience app creation as a team. This allowed me to delve into both the practical and personal parts of working as a team, requiring me to coordinate, discuss, and also learn to use collaborative tools such as git. I also enjoyed my experience as it opened my eyes to how technical know-how alone is not enough, but an all-rounded design with good considerations is required for any product to be functional and useful to people.
