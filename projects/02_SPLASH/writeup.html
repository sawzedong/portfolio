Overview of the Competition

Splash Awards is a national technology innovation competition, intended for students to showcase prototypes or ideas for certain projects. In 2021, the theme for the Splash Awards challenge was "How will Digital Intelligence & Cloud transform Singapore in a Post-Pandemic World?".

Issue Identified

My team had noticed that with the increasing trend of online shopping due to COVID-19, e-commerce scams became more common and widespread. While we recognised existing solutions such as withholding payment until the goods were marked as received, we also acknowledged the possibility of abuse of such safeguards.

Proposed Solution

As such, we decided on developing a website that had more scam protection methods built in. Our project was titled SafeShop.

Through the use of AI, listings were scanned for malicious activity and appropriate action was taken to warn buyers and restrict sellers. This is a preventative solution, allowing us to intercept such scams before they happen and is also more accurate than simple filters.

Additionally, we proposed the use of blockchain technology to store the transaction information. Being immutable, it ensures that both parties have a receipt of transaction such that there can be no false calls of fraud.

Achievements and Reflections

In the end, we were awarded a special award for Winner of Cybersecurity Theme. This competition reminded me of the importance of cybersecurity, and the implications it can have on people's lives if this is not paid attention to.

Through this competition, I had the chance to better understand the various applications of AI in helping to improve people's lives. This competition helped me to better understand the iterative idea development progress, identifying issues to then create and refine solutions. It also emphasised how solutions must always be considered with the users in mind, allowing me to consider what design or features would be the most of value to these users whom we are trying to protect.

While this competition was more theoretical and idea-based, I still had the chance to also develop out some parts of the project (such as creating a blockchain transaction). I would still hope to have more opportunity to explore more in AI, training models to develop this idea into fruition.
