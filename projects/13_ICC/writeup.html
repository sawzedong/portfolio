Why I ran for Chairperson

Ever since Secondary 1, when I joined Infocomm Club as my CCA, I had also looked up to and felt inspired by my chairperson. The Executive Committee (EXCO) and chairperson had the role of planning the general direction and programme for the year, running many lessons and activities. Many of the lessons I have had grew both my soft and hard skills greatly, and I had truly enjoyed my experience and community in the CCA.

Hence, in Secondary 3 (2021), I ran for chairperson for the Secondary branch of Infocomm Club. In JC1 (2023), I once again ran for chairperson at the JC branch of STEAM Collective (Infocomm).

Leadership in Secondary Branch

After I took on the role, I was tasked with leading my EXCO in planning training sessions for my CCA mates, while also helping to plan any major events (such as CCA selection for Sec 1s, Open House and more).

In my 1 year-long journey as chairperson, I was able to teach my CCA mates on topics such as Web Design and Competitive Programming, planning lessons that both the EXCO and the juniors were interested in. I was able to also work with a close-knit team and we greatly enjoyed our time. After learning on the difficult concepts myself, I could challenge myself to explain it in a simple manner that my juniors could also understand.

Leadership in JC Branch

Eventually, Robotics and Infocomm merged to form STEAM Collective, but maintained as separate branches with separate EXCOs but greater collaboration between the two. As chairperson in the JC branch, I had the chance to plan activities to facilitate greater understanding between both branches, bridging the gap between software and hardware.

In JC, there was also a different perspective of the CCA. With the presence of a Computing subject, our CCA could not deliver just content, but had to value add to our members. We also had to balance the tricky timetables of many members. As such, working with my EXCO, we came up with a project-based model, allowing students to dabble in the whole process of developing ideas into products.

Reflections

Overall, I learnt a lot about the practical side of leadership, such as administrative work, planning, and my own technical mastery. Yet, my greatest takeaway was from the great differences in what appeared to be similar positions, highlighting the need to truly understand those under me. As a leader, I must be ready to accommodate to deliver on what the CCA posits its own value to be, making it meaningful and fun for the members of Infocomm Club.
