Introduction to DSO World of Science

Under the Young Defence Scientists Programme (YDSP), DSO National Laboratories (DSO) hosts the World of Science (WOS) every year. It is a series of week-long workshops, meant to expose students to a wide spectrum of STEM topics with both theoretical lessons and hands-on sessions. I participated in WOS in both 2022 and 2023.

2022: Computer Security

In 2022, I selected the Computer Security module in order to further increase my knowledge of the field of cybersecurity and its related threats. Through the WOS module, I was exposed to a variety of types of digital security, such as web, mobile and hardware security. I was also able to try some hands-on with the software provided to better understand the threats in the field, such as Wireshark, binary analysis software, and many more.

The module concluded with a Capture the Flag competition, where we could put our skills to the test. We made use of our own existing knowledge and skills, as well as the tools that we had been taught throughout the course.

Overall, this activity added much fun to my 2022 June holidays, giving me the opportunity to find out more about computer security while allowing me to also interact with the given softwares. I was also able to better apply these concepts to understanding how different hackers may utilise methods to exploit vulnerabilities, and the importance of safeguarding these systems.

2023: Electromagnetics

In 2023, I selected electromagnetics to explore a different area of STEM. I was intrigued by the various devices and capabilities they had mentioned, and this led me to choose electromagnetics.

There were many hands-on opportunities provided during this module. We investigated what exactly electromagnetic waves were, what their purposes were, and how they may be manipulated or jammed. Throughout this module, we tried things like building a mini Faraday cage and Gauss rifle magnetic linear accelerator.

This activity was also interesting, helping me to better understand the practical applications of electromagnetics, a topic that is covered heavily in theory in school. While the exact concepts may not be something I apply often, it sparked a sense of curiosity for me to find out more about and also consider its various practical applications.
