Overview of the Competition

In 2020-2023, I participated in the Singapore National Olympiad of Informatics (NOI). While the competition format changed slightly over the years, the objectives of the competition remained the same. NOI is a competitive programming competition modelled after the International Olympiad of Informatics. The competition generally consisted of a preliminary and a final round. At both rounds, programming tasks were posed to challenge both algorithmic development and understanding, as well as technical progrmaming ability. The competition emphasised on not just correctness, but also efficiency of the solutions against large test cases.

Preparation for the Competition

I began preparing for NOI in mid-2019, where I attended a C++ course at the NUS School of Computing in order to improve my skills for NOI. Since then, it has sparked my interest to learn more about competitive programming as it was a good platform to not only practise my programming skills, but also to build strong algorithmic fundamentals.

I found the challenge of solving the problems and the thrill of being able to see unique solutions to be meaningful. Hence, I sought to train myself such as through online courses and making the most out of CCA sessions.

Achievements at the Competition

During NOI 2020, although the problems were difficult, I was still able to use my newfound knowledge to make my best attempts. Overall, I achieved a Bronze Medal and specially given the Youngest Medallist award.

Since then, I have also participated in 2022 and 2023 and managed to attain the Silver award.

Reflections on the Competition

NOI has truly challenged my ability to think creatively, logically, and develop solutions to difficult problems. The first step of every task was often to reason, sometimes even on pen and paper, with myself to understand the approach that I wanted to take towards each question. I also had to come to terms with my own limitations and recognise the constraints imposed by the time limit, balancing between attempting complete solutions for certain problems while brute forcing or attempting partial solutions for other problems.

There was the requirement of using complex programming concepts, and it allowed me to appreciate how programming skills alone is never sufficient, but must be paired with a good grasp on how to utilise programming to achieve a certain goal. While I believe that I still have a long way to go in terms of all these algorithmic concepts, I was glad to have this experience to push myself to learn more and to put it into practice as well.
