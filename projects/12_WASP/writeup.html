Introduction to the Project

While efforts have been made to treat Singapore's wastewater, the energy demand and environmental impact are high as both industrial and household wastewater increases. As such, as part of a team of four, we developed a Wastewater Advanced Sustainable Pre-treatment (WASP) system, utilising more natural methods for greater safety and energy efficiency.

Our project, fully titled "WASP: Novel metal oxide nanomaterial photo-electrocatalyst of pollutant breakdown and bio-phytoremediation of wastewater", proposes the use of three phases to pre-treat wastewater before the next phase using natural methods to remove certain pollutants: Metal Oxide nanomaterials Beneficial bacteria (bioremediation) Selected plants (phytoremediation)

Our Prototype Building Process

Having identified the various methods and stages we would like, we then entered into a phase of research. We collected water from various locations, adding contaminants or pollutants as needed. We then ran experiments on each phase to determine their effectiveness at removing pollutants.

Through this, we found that heavy metals and organic compounds were readily removed. Furthermore, the processes were repeatable as the metal oxides barely degraded and the bacteria and plants thrived in these environments, converting the pollutants into their own nutrients. This makes it sustainable for repeated use.

We then decided to complete the project by building a complete prototype. Tubes were used to connect water flow between the stages, where I set up an Arduino microcontroller and code to control the water pumps. We then implemented the various stages in the prototype.

Engineering Innovation Challenge (EIC)

The EIC is held annually by the Institute of Engineers, Singapore, and is open to students to showcase their projects and prototypes. Our team's project was inspired by EIC, which led us to identify an existing problem and create a prototype. After submitting our paper, going through an interview and a prototype showcase, we clinched Merit at the EIC. Furthermore, we were invited to showcase our prototype at the World Engineers' Summit from 8-9 November 2023.

International Elementz Fair

We were also encouraged by our teacher mentor to submit the project for the International Elementz Fair (IEF). After submitting our poster and video introduction, we were also invited to the final round of judging at ASRJC. We were subsequently awarded the Gold medal.

Reflections

Overall, the engineering aspect made it a rather fun and different experience. Being able to not just describe, but build my proposed idea into a somewhat functional prototype was a good experience. I enjoyed tinkering around with the hardware, electronics, and even the software to ensure a smooth flow for the prototype. It also helped me better appreciate the importance of translating ideas into actual products.
