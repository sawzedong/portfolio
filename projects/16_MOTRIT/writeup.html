My Research Experience

Having experienced various research projects, I was interested in exploring more about the field of research, particularly in computing fields. As such, I joined Science Society, which gave me the opportunity to complete a one-year research project under the guidance of a mentor. Hence, as part of a team of two, I embarked on this journey under a mentor from the A*STAR Institute of High Performance Computing.

Initially, our project faced many difficulties. We began by focusing on the topic of metasurfaces. We learnt more about their basic physical concepts, their production and how computing has augmented our simulations and understanding of it. We also explored neural nano-optics, whereby artificial intelligence process the data, correcting chromatic aberrations for sharp images. Yet, we encountered many difficulties, whereby our understanding of its principles may not have been deep enough and we struggled to reproduce the results of existing works.

Eventually, we decided to pivot our focus to optical thin-films. We were better able to replicate the results of existing works and understand its function, allowing us to then focus on using AI to augment these works. In the end, we developed Multilayer Optical Thin-films Interactive Tool assisted by Generative Pre-trained Transformers (MOTRIT-GPT).

Brief Introduction to MOTRIT-GPT

Optical thin-films are films with selective properties (reflection, absorption, transmission) at specific wavelengths. These have many purposes, such as better colour visualisation during endoscopies and energy-saving windows which reflect UV and Infrared while transmitting visible light. The design process is computationally-solved but still very time consuming. To beginners, navigating the different algorithms is difficult.

Hence, we propose the use of a Large-Language Model (LLM) to act as an agent to aid the user. Through iterative conversation, it collects the necessary parameters to trigger algorithms on the backend. We implemented functions to visualise the thin-film, simulate its properties at various wavelengths, as well as use inverse design to optimise the thin-film for an intended pattern. It then shows the results to the user.

Additionally, it is also able to answer users' questions regarding thin film and computational design. It does so using a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system, analysing the users' input to draw relevant information from the knowledge base to supplement its answer.

Singapore Science and Engineering Fair (SSEF)

We presented our work, "MOTRIT-GPT: LLMs-based Interactive Tool for Multilayer Optical Thin-films Research" at the SSEF. We were able to submit our paper, enter the finals, and present our poster to judges. Many judges commented on the usefulness of this work, and we were eventually awarded the Gold Medal and Poster Award for Science Communication.

IEEE Region 10 Conference 2024

We also attempted to submit our paper to for publication in a scientific journal. While we were unsuccessful in this endeavour, we were able to have it successfully submitted at the IEEE Region 10 Conference 2024 (IEEE TENCON). For the conference, we focused more on its inverse design capability, submitting a paper titled "Knowledge-based Extraction for Inverse Design of Optical Thin-Films".

I was very grateful to have the opportunity to be a participant and presented for our paper at the IEEE TENCON. At the conference, I was able to learn more about various applications of AI across a very diverse range of fields. More importantly, I even saw how these applications were contextualised to a person's unique field, culture or country. It was fascinating to see the ideas, inspiration, research process, and passion to develop solutions using new technology for important problems.

For myself, I also had a great opportunity throughout the whole journey. I was able to learn more technical skills and better understand the research process when it involves computing and such coding problems. Using my current abilities to develop the website and optimise the prompt for the agent were some things I felt were useful. Furthermore, writing the report and presenting it at various opportunities honed my thought processes in pitching and answering questions. Overall, it was a fun journey!
