Overview of International Mathematical Modelling Competition (IMMC)

IMMC is a competition held annually. Under the competition, participants, in a team of four, are given five days to come up with a mathematical model and solution to the problem posed. The problems posed are generally of a larger scale and may also be subjective in nature (e.g. boarding a plane, who is the greatest athlete). Hence, the challenge is to use sound reasoning to quantitatively come up with a justifiable conclusion.

The competition happens at two rounds. Teams in Singapore first compete at the national level. Two papers are shortlisted to represent Singapore at the international level.

My Experience

In 2023, the problem was titled "Using Land: A valuable resource". It aimed to find the optimal use of a plot of land, balancing community values, business profits, and sustainability. Additional tasks included analysing the impact of new developments nearby to understand how that would impact our model and outcome. A complete copy of the problem sheet is available below.

While we had received training on various methods to consider, the scale of the problem made it difficult to start. However, we eventually made progress by first drawing upon our geography knowledge, focusing on Social, Environmental and Economic pillars, before scoping what each pillar meant.

With a rough model in mind, we then spent hours looking at literature to find relevant data and statistics, evaluating each option in comparison to others. The use of programmed models and graphs aided us in this process, minimising the manual calculations we had to do. We kept clear tabs on the simplifications and assumptions we had to make, allowing us to make the problem manageable while also addressing these in our report.

While we chose somewhat arbitrary factors and weightages at the start, conducting senstivity analyses showed minimal uncertainties. Furthermore, we also analysed the impacts of a new development nearby, rationalising its impacts on our model and ultimate final selection. These gave us confidence in the robustness of the model, that it was not overfitted to just one specific scenario.

Reflections

In the end, our team was awarded High Distinction at the national level, being selected to represent Singapore. At the international level, our paper achieved an Honourable Mention.

This competition helped me to appreciate the inter-connectivity of subjects, allowing me to utilise geographical knowledge and concepts. This helped me to better visualise what exactly social and environmental impacts may look like.

While the model itself was a important, it was even more crucial that we clearly mentioned the assumptions and limitations of our model. This allowed people to further understand how it could be refined, and considerations when using the model.
